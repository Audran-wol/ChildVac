Newborn Vaccination

Essential for early immunity, newborn vaccination helps protect infants from serious diseases.

  • Provides immunity against common infant diseases.
  • Administered shortly after birth for immediate protection.
  • Follow-up doses are scheduled to ensure long-term immunity.

Toddler Shots

Toddler shots are crucial for preventing numerous childhood illnesses and ensuring healthy development.

  • Protects against measles, mumps, and rubella.
  • Essential for early childhood immunity.
  • Recommended by pediatricians for all toddlers.

Family Immunization

Family immunization is key to protecting loved ones of all ages from preventable diseases.

  • Provides vaccines for children, adults, and seniors.
  • Ensures all family members are up-to-date with their vaccinations.
  • Helps maintain public health and prevent disease outbreaks.

Child Flu Shots

Child flu shots are essential to protect young children during flu season.

  • Recommended annually for children.
  • Helps prevent the spread of flu in schools and homes.
  • Administered by healthcare professionals.

Booster Vaccines

Booster vaccines are important for maintaining immunity against diseases over time.

  • Essential for extending protection from childhood vaccines.
  • Recommended for adults and children at regular intervals.
  • Administered by healthcare professionals to ensure safety.

Why are vaccinations important for my child?

Vaccinations protect your child from serious diseases like measles, polio, and whooping cough. They also help prevent the spread of these diseases to others.

Are vaccines safe for my child?

Yes, most vaccines are safe for your child. However, there are some exceptions. For example, the measles vaccine is not safe for children under 2 years of age.
